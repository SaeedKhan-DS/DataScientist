Heart disease detection using supervised machine learning

In this project, we explored the best-performing machine learning algorithm for a small, limited dataset of 300 patients. Multiple models—including KNN, SVM, Decision Trees, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost—were implemented and evaluated. While all algorithms demonstrated strong performance, XGBoost outperformed the rest, achieving an impressive 87% accuracy and 89% sensitivity.
The results highlight XGBoost's robustness and efficiency in handling limited medical data, making it an excellent choice for predictive modeling where high sensitivity and accuracy are critical. This success underscores the potential of ensemble methods in small-scale, high-stakes datasets.
